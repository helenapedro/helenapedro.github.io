Free Hydrocarbons

The data presented in this work refers to studies of organic geochemistry, carried out on samples that were collected in the Cabo Ledo formation (onshore of the Kwanza Basin). The general objective of the work was to evaluate free hydrocarbons. The methodology used implied descriptive, quantitative and qualitative research of the bibliographical and theoretical-experimental type. The results obtained demonstrated that the obtained free hydrocarbons (S1) (from 0 to 0.5% mg of HC/g TOC) indicate that such mother rocks generated small amounts of free hydrocarbons. However, the data on the total organic carbon (TOC) - from 2.09 to 3.48 mg of HC/g COT and the levels of hydrocarbons to be generated (S2) - from 19.1 to 272 mg of HC/g COT indicate a high generation potential. Rock-Eval pyrolysis data pointed to type I cerogen that contains organic matter of lacustrine and marine origin. From this research, it was concluded that this is the type of organic matter that most generates hydrocarbons with a conversion factor of 85 to 90% and that oil is the main product expelled at its peak of maturation. The maximum temperature data (Tmax) show that the organic matter is predominantly in its early-mature phase with values ranging from 435 to 445°C. Thus, some recommendations were given regarding the need for companies that have provided services in this region to make their material available for consultation, as well as the need to carry out more in-depth studies on the subject..
