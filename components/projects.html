Detecting Fraud In Stock Data

The project focuses on detecting fraudulent activities and identifying suspicious stock behavior within stock data for a hedge fund.
Utilizing the yfinance library, data extraction was conducted, resulting in a pandas dataframe.
Fraud, in this context, refers to deceitful or criminal actions intended to generate financial gain.
Historical stock prices were obtained using the history() method, with the period parameter determining the data timeframe.
Additionally, dividends were analyzed to assess profit distribution to shareholders.
No evident signs of fraud were identified in the stock data.
Stock prices fluctuate based on factors such as outstanding shares, future profits, and market demand, with trading activity continuously reported via stock tickers.

Car Price Data Analysis

The objective of this project was to analyze car price data to identify key variables influencing car prices.

Questions addressed included examining the relationship between drive systems and vehicle prices, and whether engine size could predict car prices.

Descriptive statistics, scatter plots, heatmaps, and correlation analyses were employed.

Results revealed that rear-wheel drive convertibles and hardtops commanded the highest prices, while 4WD hatchbacks had the lowest value.

Analysis indicated a positive linear relationship between engine size and price.

Key variables for predicting car prices included various continuous numerical factors such as length, width, engine size, and horsepower, along with categorical variables like drive wheels.

Data preprocessing steps and analytical methods are outlined, with insights drawn from groupby analysis, heatmaps, and correlation studies.

National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES) Data Analysis

The primary objective of this project was to investigate differences in blood pressure and educational attainment based on age, aiming to determine if aging directly influences blood pressure.

Various analytical techniques, including frequency tables, numerical summaries, individual summary statistics, and graphical summaries, were employed.

Results revealed disparities in both blood pressure and educational attainment across different age groups.

While aging appears to correlate with increased blood pressure, educational attainment demonstrates a "birth cohort effect."

Younger individuals exhibit higher educational attainment due to changing trends over decades, with the prevalence of college education rising over time. As these cohorts age, the relationship between age and educational attainment in cross-sectional data evolves accordingly.

The project utilized data from the NHANES cross-sectional survey, collected at a single point in time, highlighting the dynamic interplay between age, blood pressure, and educational attainment.
