IMPACT OF CRUDE OIL PRICES IN THE INTERNATIONAL MARKET ON ANGOLAN ECONOMY

Excel | Line/Area Chart | t-Test

The main objective of this project was to assess the impact of crude oil prices in the international market on the Angolan economy. For this purpose, data on GDP Per Capita Growth and Crude Oil Prices over several years were analyzed.

The period from 2005 to 2008 was precisely the one that registreted the highest GDP growth rates in Angola. There was a sharp drop during the years 2008 and 2009, which coincides with the subprime financial crisis in the United States of America and the fall in world GDP growth and in the main developed and emerging economies.

The data show that the Angolan economy was twice affected by the fall in the price of a barrel of oil, once in 1998 and once in 2008/2009. The present crisis was the third, after the country's independence.

The data suggest that the "mini-golden" age of the country's economic growth (2002 / 2008) presents a strong correlation with the price and oil revenues, which is no longer identifiable with the period between 2011 and 2019.

FREE HYDROCARBONS

Power BI | Excel

The data presented in this work refers to studies of organic geochemistry, carried out on samples that were collected in the Cabo Ledo formation (onshore of the Kwanza Basin). The general objective of the work was to evaluate free hydrocarbons.

The results obtained demonstrated that the obtained free hydrocarbons (S1) indicate that such mother rocks generated small amounts of free hydrocarbons. However, the data on the total organic carbon (TOC) and the levels of hydrocarbons to be generated (S2) indicate a high generation potential. Rock-Eval pyrolysis data pointed to type I cerogen that contains organic matter of lacustrine and marine origin.

From this research, it was concluded that this is the type of organic matter that most generates hydrocarbons with a conversion factor of 85 to 90% and that oil is the main product expelled at its peak of maturation.

The maximum temperature data (Tmax) show that the organic matter is predominantly in its early-mature phase with values ranging from 435 to 445°C.

REAL STATE DATA ANALYSIS

Excel | Hypothesis Test | Histogram | Line Chart | t-Test | ANOVA | Pairwise Correlation

Analysis of the dataset from the Department of Housing and Urban Development (DHDU) of USA to determine differences in fair market rent over 5 years.

Difference in the Market Values is significant only for years 2005 and 2011. In these years the market value of 'Occupied' units was greater than 'Not-Occupied' units. For the remaining years there is no significant difference in the market value across 'Occupied' and 'Not-Occupied' units.

The pattern discernable is that the Market value of 'Occupied' units is never less than that for 'Not-Occupied' units. It is either greater (as in years 2005 and 2011) or equal (as in the remaining years).

As seen by the various statistical tests (t-tests for differences in means) it can be seen that the Fair Market Rents continuously rose across these various years. The highest increase percentage across years was observed from 2007 to 2009, the period overlapping the subprime mortgage crisis.

The R-square and adjusted R-square are about 0.52, indicating that the model explains about 52 percentage of variation in the market value of housing units.
